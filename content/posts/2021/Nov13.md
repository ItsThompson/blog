An irrational number is a number that cannot be expressed at a simple fraction. However, how can we prove that √2 is a irrational number when we don't know its true value? This is where mathematical proofs come in [Mathematical Proofs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_proof) come in. Mathematical proofs are arguments that use logical reasoning to prove the conclusion is valid.

### Proving √2 is irrational:

#### Step 1, Selecting a proving method:

We can't quite prove that √2 is irrational, so let us prove the opposite. This method is called [Proof by Contradiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proof_by_contradiction). Hence we can create the following assumption:

> "√2 is rational"

![Selecting a proving method](https://i.imgur.com/MfBcNxe.jpg)

#### Step 2, Define what √2 is:

> √2 = a/b

where a∈Z and b∈Z, b≠0, a&b are relatively prime. Relatively prime means that numbers share no common positive factors except 1.
